About Sapporo City

Sapporo is the largest city in Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost island. It is the fifth-largest city in Japan by population and the capital of Hokkaido Prefecture. The city has a population of over 1.9 million people and is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, rich history, and unique culture.

History of Sapporo City

Sapporo was first settled in the late 1800s and quickly grew into a thriving city. In 1972, Sapporo hosted the Winter Olympics, which helped to boost its international profile. Today, the city is a popular destination for tourists who come to enjoy its stunning scenery and unique culture.

Food in Sapporo City

Sapporo is famous for its cuisine, which includes fresh seafood, Hokkaido dairy products, and miso ramen. Some popular dishes to try include:

  • Miso Ramen
  • Sapporo-style Jingisukan (grilled lamb)
  • Ishikari Nabe (a seafood hot pot)
